Artists

Steve McCurry

Steve McCurry is an American photographer renowned for his captivating portraiture and compelling documentation of global cultures.

Best known for his iconic “Afghan Girl” portrait, his work often captures moments of resilience and humanity in regions marked by conflict and hardship.

McCurry’s photographs, rich in color and emotion, tell powerful stories that transcend borders, making him one of the most celebrated photojournalists of his generation.

Fishermen, Weligama

Digital C-Type Print

1995

Steve McCurry’s photograph Fishermen, Weligama, South Coast, Sri Lanka, is a stunning visual narrative that captures the unique tradition of stilt fishing, a practice native to this coastal region. The image creates a serene yet dynamic scene, highlighting the vibrant blues of the ocean against the warm tones of the fishermen's clothing and the elements surrounding them.

Holi Man

Digital C-Type Print

1996

Steve McCurry’s Holi Man is a vivid representation of the colorful and exuberant festival of Holi in India. The photograph features a man covered in vibrant powders, a hallmark of the celebration, which symbolizes the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. McCurry’s masterful use of color and light brings the subject to life, making the viewer feel the joy and vibrancy of this cultural event.

Flower Vendor on Dal Lake

Digital C-Type Print

1999

Steve McCurry’s photograph Flower Vendor on Dal Lake beautifully encapsulates the vibrant life and culture surrounding Dal Lake in Kashmir. The image features a flower vendor surrounded by a stunning array of colorful blooms, set against the serene backdrop of the lake and its iconic houseboats. McCurry’s use of rich colors and textures draws the viewer into the intimate connection between the vendor and the natural beauty of his surroundings.

Clover Gatherers

Digital C-Type Print

1999

Steve McCurry’s Clover Gatherers, taken in Yemen, is a captivating glimpse into the daily lives of local workers in the Yemeni countryside. The photograph features a group of men engaged in the traditional practice of gathering clover, showcasing their connection to the land and the simplicity of rural life. McCurry’s keen eye for detail captures the lush greenery and the textures of the clover, while the expressions of the gatherers evoke a sense of camaraderie and purpose.

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Digital C-Type Print

2007

Steve McCurry’s photograph Fushimi Inari Shrine, taken in Kyoto, Japan, captures the serene beauty and spiritual significance of this iconic site. The image showcases the mesmerizing pathway lined with thousands of vibrant vermilion torii gates, which lead visitors through a tranquil forest setting. McCurry’s ability to convey the interplay of light and shadow highlights the ethereal quality of the shrine, creating a sense of depth and inviting viewers to step into the scene.

About the artist

Steve McCurry is an iconic American photographer renowned for his stunning portraiture and compelling storytelling through imagery. Born in 1950, McCurry gained global fame with his famous photograph, Afghan Girl, which was featured on the cover of National Geographic in 1985. His work captures the human experience across cultures and geographies, often highlighting the resilience and beauty of people in challenging circumstances. McCurry's use of vibrant colors, rich textures, and emotional depth has made him one of the most celebrated photographers in contemporary visual culture, earning him numerous accolades and awards throughout his career.


McCurry's extensive travels and deep engagement with diverse cultures have resulted in a vast portfolio that spans conflict zones, remote villages, and bustling cities. He emphasizes the power of photography as a tool for empathy and understanding, encouraging viewers to connect with the subjects of his work on a personal level. Through his exhibitions and publications, McCurry continues to inspire audiences worldwide, reminding us of the shared humanity that binds us all, regardless of our differences. His legacy is defined by his ability to evoke emotion and provoke thought, solidifying his place as a master storyteller through the lens.
