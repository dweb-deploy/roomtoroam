Artists

John Kline

John Kline, a Denver-based artist, blends abstract and figurative elements to examine the complexities of the human experience.

Known for his striking use of bold colors and layered techniques, his work explores themes such as memory, emotion, and personal growth.

Starting as an educator and now a full time artist, Kline relocated to Denver in 2023 to continue to develop his artistic practice.

Paradise Lost

Enamel/Oil on Canvas

2021

"Paradise Lost" masterfully captures the essence of contrasting realms—innocence and experience—through a vivid interplay of color and form. This striking piece invites viewers to explore the complex themes of beauty and melancholy, drawing inspiration from both nature and human emotion. Kline employs a blend of acrylic and oil techniques, creating layers that evoke depth and movement.

Archaic Apollo

Oil/Markers on Canvas

2022

"Archaic Apollo" evokes the essence of Apollo, the Greek god of light and arts, while simultaneously exploring themes of renewal and the cyclical nature of existence. Kline's use of color and form encourages a dialogue between the viewer and the piece, reflecting both the serenity and complexity inherent in the human experience.

Fan

Ink on Bristol

2021

"Fan" showcases Kline's unique approach to drawing, often characterized by surreal elements and a striking use of pen and ink. This piece employs meticulous detail and a thoughtful composition to create themes of identity, perception, and the mundane aspects of everyday life, which lead the viewer to relax and reflect.

Lacy

Oil on Canvas

2021

"Lacy" exemplifies Kline's unique approach to blending modern expressionism with surrealist influences. "Lacy" showcases delicate yet intense brushwork, revealing layers of texture and depth that invite the viewer to engage emotionally. His choice of colors—often bold yet harmonious—accentuates the underlying themes of the piece, while the intricate interplay between form and abstraction adds to its complexity. Like much of Kline’s work, "Lacy" captures the essence of human experience through a lens that feels both deeply personal and universally resonant.

Last Goodbye

Oil on Canvas

2021

In "Last Goodbye", John Kline presents a poignant exploration of themes related to loss and farewell. The artwork captures a moment that resonates with deep emotional significance, reflecting the bittersweet nature of saying goodbye to someone or something meaningful. Through his characteristic style, Kline employs a rich color palette and intricate details, creating an atmosphere that evokes both nostalgia and acceptance.

About the artist

John Kline is a Denver-based visual artist whose work explores the human condition through abstract and figurative forms. With a background in fine arts and illustration, Kline’s style is marked by his use of vibrant colors and layered textures, which create a sense of depth and movement. His pieces often reflect themes of identity, emotion, and transformation. He has exhibited widely, with a focus on engaging audiences by encouraging personal interpretation and connection with his artwork. Kline started his career as an art educator before gaining popularity for his work and transitioning to a full time artist.


His current work centers around depictions of past memories and moments of love, heartbreak, death, abandonment, loneliness, joy, and disappointment. Some of his pieces also explore personal fantasies and desires. Much of his artwork features linear lines influenced by comic illustration, used to express recollections and emotions related to these memories. The process of creating these works serves as a way for him to understand the impact these events had on his life and as a method of recovery from feelings of guilt, anxiety, and depression.
