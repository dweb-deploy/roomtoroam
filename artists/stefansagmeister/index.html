Artists

Stefan Sagmeister

Stefan Sagmeister is an Austrian-born designer known for his bold, thought-provoking work that blurs the lines between graphic design and art.

As co-founder of Sagmeister & Walsh, he has developed influential projects that explore themes of happiness, beauty, and human nature.

Sagmeister’s work challenges traditional design boundaries, making him a leading figure in contemporary visual culture and a frequent speaker on the emotional impact of design.

Set the Twilight Reeling (album cover)

Photography, Graphic Design

1996

Stefan Sagmeister’s cover art for Set the Twilight Reeling, Lou Reed's 1996 album, is a notable example of his experimental and emotive approach to design. Sagmeister’s portrait of Reed on the cover is striking in its simplicity and intensity, presenting the rock icon in a direct, almost confrontational pose. By focusing closely on Reed's face, Sagmeister emphasizes the artist's raw and unfiltered presence.

OK Go (album cover)

Graphic Design

2002

Stefan Sagmeister’s design for OK Go, the debut album from the band of the same name, is an emblematic example of his unconventional and playful approach to album art. Sagmeister created a cover that reflected the band’s energetic, offbeat style, utilizing vibrant colors and a layered, textured appearance that mirrors the eclectic, fun nature of OK Go's music.

Chaumont

Lithography, Typography

2004

Stefan Sagmeister’s Chaumont poster, designed for the renowned Chaumont International Poster Festival, showcases his signature fusion of art, typography, and personal expression. This piece embodies Sagmeister's unconventional approach, using bold visuals and a tactile, textured design that breaks from traditional poster aesthetics. Sagmeister created an intricate typographic composition, often blending humor, depth, and visual surprise.

Take it On - SVA

Offset Lithography

2013

Stefan Sagmeister’s Take it On poster series for the School of Visual Arts (SVA) is an iconic example of his adventurous and personal approach to design. Created to inspire students, these posters boldly feature Sagmeister’s own body with motivational phrases written directly onto his skin, representing the idea of embracing challenges and making a lasting impact through creative work.

Now is Better

Book, Non-Fiction

2023

Stefan Sagmeister's latest work is a book titled Now is Better, which presents optimistic data visualizations that challenge common negative perceptions about the world. This project builds on Sagmeister's interest in both design and data, as he creatively transforms factual statistics into abstract, visual narratives that suggest an improving global situation.

About the artist

Stefan Sagmeister is an Austrian-born graphic designer and typographer celebrated for his daring, provocative approach to design. Known for pushing creative boundaries, Sagmeister’s work often combines bold typography, striking visuals, and experimental concepts that leave a lasting impact. After founding Sagmeister Inc., he created iconic designs for clients such as the Rolling Stones, HBO, and the Guggenheim Museum. His work is characterized by a willingness to explore unconventional mediums and techniques, often challenging societal norms and exploring themes of happiness, personal growth, and human connection.


In addition to his client work, Sagmeister is known for his introspective art projects, including *The Happy Show* and his book *Things I Have Learned in My Life So Far*, where he reflects on personal insights through visual storytelling. He frequently lectures and exhibits internationally, sharing his ideas on design’s power to evoke emotion and spark conversation. Sagmeister’s influence extends far beyond traditional graphic design, inspiring creatives around the world to approach their work with authenticity and boldness.
