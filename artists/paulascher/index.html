Paula Scher

Paula Scher is an acclaimed American graphic designer and educator known for her innovative approach to typography and branding.

As a partner at Pentagram, she has created iconic visual identities for organizations such as the Public Theater and Citibank, blending bold aesthetics with effective communication.

Scher's work often reflects her passion for urban culture and art, making her a leading figure in contemporary graphic design.

Boston (album cover)

Graphic Design

1976

Paula Scher's cover art for the Boston album, released in 1976, is a quintessential example of her bold, graphic design style that melds typography with striking visual elements. The cover features a vivid, abstract depiction of the band's name and artwork that encapsulates the energetic essence of their music. Scher's approach combines vibrant colors and dynamic shapes, creating a sense of movement that resonates with the sound of the band, making the cover instantly recognizable.

About the artist

Paula Scher is a pioneering American graphic designer and educator renowned for her influential work in typography and visual identity. Scher became one of the first female partners at the prestigious design firm Pentagram, where she has created iconic branding and environmental graphics for clients such as Citibank, The Public Theater, and the New York City Ballet. Her bold use of color, typography, and composition has transformed the landscape of graphic design, earning her recognition as a leader in the field. Scher's work often features a playful yet sophisticated aesthetic that challenges traditional design norms and communicates complex ideas with clarity.


Beyond her design practice, Scher is a passionate educator, sharing her expertise through teaching at institutions like the School of Visual Arts. She has received numerous awards, including the AIGA Medal and the National Design Award, reflecting her impact on the industry. Scher's ability to blend art and design has not only shaped corporate identities but has also inspired a generation of designers to explore the intersection of creativity and communication. Her work continues to resonate, emphasizing the importance of strong visual language in today's fast-paced, information-driven world.
