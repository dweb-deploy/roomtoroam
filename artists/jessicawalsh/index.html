Artists

Jessica Walsh

Jessica Walsh is a prominent American designer and art director, known for her innovative approach to visual communication and branding.

As a partner at the creative agency Sagmeister & Walsh, she has developed visually striking campaigns that merge art and design, pushing the boundaries of traditional media.

Walsh's work often explores themes of emotion and storytelling, making her a leading figure in contemporary graphic design and advertising.

Aizone FW12 Campaign

Bodypaint, photography

2011

For Aizone FW12, Jessica Walsh created a striking campaign that features models adorned with bold typographic body paint. This black-and-white shoot stands out for its provocative and artistic approach, transforming the human form into a canvas for expressive text and graphics. The body paint adds an intriguing layer to the visuals, showcasing a unique blend of fashion and art that challenges traditional advertising norms.

Aizone 11-14 Campaign

Bodypaint, photography

2014

Aizone 11-14 is a vibrant campaign that showcases models adorned with colorful designs and inspiring phrases such as "Trust Your Gut" and "Make Your Own Rules" painted on their faces. This series, created for Aizone, reflects a bold aesthetic that combines lively colors and motivational messages, transforming the models into living canvases that blend seamlessly with the bright backgrounds.

Aizone 14-16 Campaign

Bodypaint, graphic design, photography

2014

Jessica Walsh's work Aizone 14-16 is part of a vibrant campaign created in collaboration with Stefan Sagmeister for Aizone, a Middle Eastern department store. This particular campaign draws inspiration from the pop art movement, heavily influenced by the works of Roy Lichtenstein. It features bold colors, geometric shapes, and a playful use of polka dots and cross-hatching techniques, embodying a lively and modern aesthetic.

Frooti

Graphic design, photography

2017

Frooti showcases a vibrant rebranding campaign for the Indian mango drink, reflecting a fresh approach to connect with a younger audience. Walsh and her team traveled to India, drawing inspiration from the country's colorful advertising landscape, which they found cluttered and text-heavy. In contrast, they designed the Frooti campaign to feature bold, graphic visuals with a playful twist, utilizing a miniature world concept. This involved creating tiny models while keeping the Frooti packaging and mangoes at real-life scale to make them the focal point.

Milly: Color Lover

Graphic design, photography

2018

Milly: Color Lover captures the essence of individuality and inclusiveness through a vibrant exploration of chromatic dressing. Created for Milly's Fall 2018 campaign, this work reflects the designer Michelle Smith's vision of a polarized world, aiming to create a welcoming space for self-expression. Walsh’s creation showcases models adorned in a rainbow of colors, symbolizing unity and diversity, inviting viewers to embrace their unique identities within a collective narrative.

About the artist

Jessica Walsh is an innovative American designer, art director, and partner at the renowned design firm Sagmeister & Walsh. Born in 1986, she is recognized for her bold, visually striking work that combines elements of graphic design, typography, and photography. Walsh gained prominence for her creative collaborations and her ability to push the boundaries of traditional design. One of her notable projects is the 40 Days of Dating experiment, a social media-based narrative that explored modern relationships and garnered significant attention, leading to a bestselling book and an animated series.


Beyond her design work, Walsh is an advocate for gender equality in the creative industry and actively engages in mentorship and educational initiatives. She has been recognized with numerous awards, including the prestigious ADC Young Gun Award and has served as a speaker and juror at various design conferences. Walsh’s unique blend of creativity, social commentary, and passion for design continues to inspire a new generation of artists and designers, making her a significant figure in contemporary visual culture.
